I'm an engineer at heart, but I got here backwards. I spent eight years running a multi-state aviation business — the P&L, the people, the weather delays — before deciding to head out on walkabout. I left the director's office to go build things for a while, and it was the best decision I've ever made.

Since then, I've had the pleasure of working on technology flown on the International Space Station, technology that sends bits of data riding on light waves through the depths of space, and technology that carries light across continents. The building hasn't stopped, but it turns out the years running a business never left me either. These days I direct a large program across four engineering disciplines, lead two AI research efforts I carried from proposal through funding, and am finishing an M.S. in artificial intelligence at NJIT in whatever hours are left. Engineering is my heart; pulling teams together around hard problems is my strength.

This is the site where I write about the things that interest me.

Engineering

Manufacturing Executability [security]
A pipeline that finds memory-safety bugs in binaries with no source, then proves each one with a crash that reproduces every time. The hard part isn't the analysis — it's manufacturing a way to run code that was never meant to be run on its own.
Anatomy of a Plugin-Install RCE [disclosure]
A transcription app I use added a plugin system. Installing a plugin ran its code before checking whether it was valid, over plaintext HTTP, with no signature. A walk through the finding and the responsible-disclosure report I filed.
Sanitizer: Scrub Transcripts Before the LLM Sees Them [privacy]
A Buzz plugin that swaps names, PII, and codenames for placeholders before a transcript goes to a cloud LLM, then restores the reply. The key never leaves your machine, and if a removal can’t be confirmed, there is no output.
meowplotlib: Cats on Your Charts [python]
I published a Python package that puts cats on your matplotlib charts. One import, and every figure you save has company. The cats never sit on your data.
